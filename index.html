Scenario-based judgment

Question 05 — Scenario

She Owns the Block syndicate invests $300,000 in a development deal. Each participating member's share is $25,000. At the 18-month mark, 75% of the contract price — $225,000 — has already been paid to the developer. The developer now requests an additional 20% above the original contract price, plus 9 more months to deliver the units. If a member chooses to withdraw at this stage, they would only recover a portion of what they contributed — and the value of that money has already been reduced by 18 months of inflation, lost investment returns, and the cost of exiting a partially completed deal.

Knowing this, how do you respond?

Commitment and exit policy

Question 14 — Default consequences

In the event a member defaults on their monthly contribution, the following escalating consequence structure has been proposed. Do you agree with this approach?

1st missed month: formal warning. End of 2nd month: second warning + 10% interest on total contributions owed. End of 3rd month: 25% interest on total contributions owed. End of 4th month: immediate removal, with remaining members collectively making up the shortfall to keep the project on track.

Question 15

The club proposes the following voluntary exit policy: a member becomes eligible to voluntarily exit only after completing 9 consecutive months of contributions with no missed or late payments. Only one voluntary exit is permitted within any 12-month period. Payout will be made less any applicable taxes and expenses. Do you agree with these conditions?
